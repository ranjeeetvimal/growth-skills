# Ongoing SEO Cadence

SEO is not a one-time project — rankings decay without maintenance because algorithms
update, competitors keep moving, search intent shifts, and content goes stale. The job of
a good maintenance program is to be **proactive, not reactive**: catch drops and
opportunities before they hit the bottom line. Use this to build the "ongoing" half of the
roadmap (it's what mistake #12, "treating SEO as one-time," routes here to fix).

## The maintenance loop
A continuous cycle, not a launch:
**Track & Analyze → Evaluate → Optimize → Build & Maintain → (back to Track).**
- **Track & Analyze** — organic traffic, keyword rankings, conversions, competitor moves, algorithm changes.
- **Evaluate** — are we hitting goals? content still effective? new technical issues? intent still matched?
- **Optimize** — update decaying content, integrate new keywords, ship technical fixes.
- **Build & Maintain** — new content, backlinks, community/brand, keep the calendar moving.

## The cadence (tune the frequencies to the site's real data)
| Frequency | Do | Owner skill |
|---|---|---|
| **Weekly / continuous** | Monitor organic traffic, rankings, and conversions; a sudden drop is a signal to investigate, not wait | `seo-analytics` |
| **Monthly** | Refresh 1-2 decaying pages before writing new ones; publish + optimize new content; scan competitor moves; spot-check broken links / Core Web Vitals | `seo-content`, `technical-seo-audit` |
| **Quarterly** | Proactive **content audit** — find outdated info, content gaps, cannibalization, and pruning candidates; backlink profile review; re-check search intent on the money terms | `seo-content`, `link-building`, `keyword-research` |
| **Seasonally** | Revisit keywords for seasonal intent shifts (e.g. "beach hotel" in summer → "seasonal short breaks" in winter); adjust content and targeting | `keyword-research`, `seo-content` |
| **On a core update** | Re-check rankings, adjust; don't knee-jerk on minor updates | `technical-seo-audit`, `seo-content` |
| **Annually** | Strategy review vs. goals; future-proof for shifts — chiefly AI search now | `seo-strategy`, `ai-search-optimization` |

## Content decay & refresh (the highest-leverage recurring task)
Fresh content alone isn't enough — existing pages need ongoing optimization: internal links,
semantically related keywords, depth, meta, and freshness. **Refresh decaying pages before
writing new ones** — reclaiming a page that already ranks is usually cheaper than earning a
new one. Prioritize refreshes by traffic/revenue lost, not by age.

## Evidence discipline (applies here too)
- Track algorithm changes via the **primary source** — Google Search Central's core-updates
  page — not vendor speculation about what "the update rewarded."
- Do NOT lean on "X% of marketers do this" survey stats to justify the cadence. Popularity
  isn't evidence, and unsourced adoption numbers break the suite's evidence rule. The cadence
  above is a **starting heuristic** — tune every frequency to what the site's own traffic and
  ranking data actually show.
