# Platform-Fit Matrix

For `social-context`. Match the ICP + the founder's format strength + the goal to a platform — then
pick ONE (maybe two) and skip the rest. All platform demographics shift and vendor stats are biased;
treat everything below as a **starting heuristic to verify**, never a fixed fact.

## The one question that decides most of it
**Where does your ICP already go to discover things like yours?**
- They Google it → SEO, not social.
- They ask peers in a Slack / Discord / subreddit → communities.
- They scroll a feed for ideas → the platform that feed lives on.

Follow the buyer, not the trend.

## The matrix (heuristics — verify per niche)
| Platform | Best-fit ICP | Format strength | Buying intent | Skip if… |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **X (Twitter)** | Founders, devs, marketers, AI/tech, build-in-public | Writing, hot takes, threads | Medium — audience → email → product | ICP is local, older, or non-tech |
| **LinkedIn** | B2B buyers, execs, recruiters, services | Writing, professional POV | High for B2B leads | You sell to consumers / teens |
| **Instagram** | Consumer, lifestyle, visual products, local | Photo / short video, aesthetic | Medium — brand + shopping | Your product isn't visual |
| **TikTok** | Gen-Z / younger consumer, entertainment, discovery | Short video, personality | Low-medium, high reach | You can't or won't do video |
| **YouTube** | Anyone researching before buying; how-to niches | Long / short video, teaching | High — search + trust | No video capacity or patience (slow build) |
| **Threads / others** | Overlaps X; earlier-stage audience | Writing | Low-medium | Thin unless you're already winning on X |

## Format strength beats platform hype
A strong writer wins on X / LinkedIn and struggles on TikTok; a natural on camera should not be
forced to grind out threads. Pick the platform the founder can actually be *good* at — weekly, for
months — not the one that's trending.

## Sacrifice: why one platform first
Consistency compounds; splitting effort across three platforms usually means winning on none. Win
one platform's audience, then repurpose into a second later. Always name the 2-4 platforms to skip now.

## When organic social is the wrong channel
If the ICP mostly **searches** (→ SEO), lives in **gated communities** (→ community-led), or is a
short list of **named accounts** (→ outbound / ABM), say so — organic social may be a slow, low-fit
bet. Don't default to social just because everyone's on it.
