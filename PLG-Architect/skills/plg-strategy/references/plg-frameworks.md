# PLG Frameworks

## Macro thesis ("Why now?")
Page 1 of any strategy answers why this business exists NOW.
- **Structural shift** — what changed in the world?
- **Enabler** — what tech/behavior makes it possible now?
- **Timing window** — how long is the opening? (name months)
- **Risk to window** — what closes it?

## Quantified bet format
Every recommendation is a bet, not a task:
```
BET: [name]
What:      [one sentence]
Why now:   [the opening it exploits]
Outcome:   [specific metric change]
Confidence: High / Med / Low — why
Effort:    [days / weeks / months]
Risk:      [what makes it fail]
Validate:  [how to test in 30 days]
Sacrifice: [what we DON'T do instead]
```

## Red-team patterns (find 3 ways it dies)
- **Platform risk** — you're built on someone's API/policy/algorithm.
- **Incumbent response** — the leader copies you or cuts price.
- **Undifferentiated** — your wedge is table stakes; nobody switches.
- **Unit economics** — CAC > LTV, or free-tier cost outruns conversion.
- **Timing** — too early (market not ready) or too late (window closed).
For each: likelihood, early-warning metric, and the hedge to build now.

## Prioritization
Sequence bets by Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort, under the founder's stated
constraint (engineers, runway, the one 90-day metric).

## The bitter-truth principle
Name what's genuinely strong FIRST and specifically — earned credit buys the
right to be heard. Then state the hard truths plainly, each with its reason and
a way to test it. Threaded honesty (inline confidence + one clear "bitter truth"
callout) beats both a cheerleading deck and a gate that just says "go away and validate."
