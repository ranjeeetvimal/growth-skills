# PLG Playbook — Concrete Moves Menu

Use this to turn strategy into specific things the founder can DO. Pull the
2-4 moves that fit their model/stage. Every move = a lever, not a to-do list.
Always pair a move with its confidence and a 30-day validation.

---

## Acquisition loops (pick 1 primary)

| Loop | How it works | Best when | K-factor reality |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Branded-artifact loop** | Every output the user creates carries your brand to new eyes (Canva designs, Loom videos, "sent via X" footers) | Product produces shareable/visible output | K 0.1–0.4 — amplifier, not exponential |
| **Referral/invite loop** | User invites others for mutual benefit (credits, collab) | Product is collaborative or social | K 0.2–0.7 if incentive real |
| **Content/demo-as-funnel** | The content IS the product demo (trigger words, live demos, interactive tools) | Product is visual/instant to show | Compounds via SEO, not viral |
| **Switcher land-grab** | Capture migration intent when an incumbent stumbles (price hike, plan cut) | A big competitor just angered its base | Time-boxed; decays in 1-2 quarters |
| **Marketplace/network** | Each new user makes the product better for others | Two-sided or data-network product | True network effect if real |

**Instrument every loop:** track the loop input → visit → signup as your K-factor.
If you can't measure it, it's a story, not a loop.

## Engineering-as-marketing (free tools)
Ship a free calculator/checker/generator that your ICP shares. Examples of the
pattern: a "cost calculator" vs an incumbent, a "risk/health checker," a
"generator" for the ICP's recurring task. Built for links + social shares, not SEO alone.

## Activation levers
- **Define the aha precisely** — the first moment of real value *delivered*, not "account created." Name it.
- **TTV target** — median time-to-value under 5 min for self-serve tools. Instrument the riskiest step (OAuth, import) separately.
- **Template-first, never blank canvas** — ask "what do you want to do?" and pre-build it. Kill empty states.
- **Forced self-test ritual** — make the user trigger their own aha in onboarding ("do X now and watch it work").
- **Celebrate the aha** — confetti + reinforce the core promise at the emotional peak.

## Retention levers
- **Weekly value receipt** — email/notify what the product did for them ("we did X, saving you Y hours"). Doubles as upsell surface.
- **Habit anchored to their trigger, not daily logins** — tie re-engagement to their natural cadence.
- **Re-engagement ladder** — Day 3 (not activated → 1-click template), Day 7 (activated, not repeating → micro-lesson), Day 30 (results recap + offer).
- **Turn a constraint into a progress mechanic** — if there's a ramp/limit, reframe it as levelling up.

## Monetization levers
- **Define a PQL** (product-qualified lead): the usage signal that predicts willingness to pay (e.g. "delivered ≥100 units AND hit a paid-feature wall"). Route PQLs into upgrade prompts.
- **Ghost-data downgrade** — when a trial ends, show what they'd lose ("34 leads captured, paused"), don't just cut it.
- **Usage-triggered upgrade** — surface the paid feature contextually at the moment of need, not in a pricing page.
- **Honest scarcity** — founding-member pricing with a REAL date, never open-ended fake urgency.
- **Second-act pricing** — a single low tier caps ARPU; plan expansion SKUs (analytics, multi-seat, AI) for 6-12 mo. Charge for intelligence/scale, not the core promise.

## Breaking a revenue ceiling (when that's the founder's fear)
A low price × a narrow segment = a low ceiling — that's arithmetic, not strategy.
The three levers, in order of leverage: (1) **ARPU** — a higher-value second tier or
usage-based add-on for the power users who already hit limits; (2) **conversion** — a
sharper PQL and paywall placement; (3) **segment width** — expand the beachhead only
AFTER you own it. Model which lever the ceiling actually needs (see growth-metrics'
ceiling check) — usually it's ARPU, not more users.

## The "what NOT to do" discipline
For every 3 moves you recommend, name 5 the founder should NOT do now, with the
reason. Sacrifice is the strategy. Common pre-PMF sacrifices: paid ads (before
PQL→paid is proven), multi-platform expansion, cold outreach, enterprise features,
SEO on high-competition head terms.
