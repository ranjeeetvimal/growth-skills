# ICP Frameworks

## Desperate Customer Test
The desperate customer: (1) has the problem TODAY, (2) has tried and failed to
solve it, (3) has budget or time, (4) is actively searching, (5) would be ANGRY
if you vanished. If the founder can't point to 10 real people who fit all 5, the
ICP is a hypothesis — say so, then help them find the segment that does.

## "Too broad" test
More than 2 of these = too broad: multiple industries, company sizes, use cases,
channels, price points. "Everyone" is not an ICP — it's the incumbent's market and
it causes the low-ceiling problem founders fear. Narrow to ONE beachhead defined by
the JOB ("I do X and can't keep up"), not an identity. The beachhead is a doorway,
not a cage — it's how you earn the right to expand later.

## Draft personas AS HYPOTHESES (don't make the founder invent them)
From the product + category, propose 2-4 personas as testable hypotheses, clearly
labeled unvalidated. Use real category behavior + public signals. Card format:
```
PERSONA: "[Name]" — [the archetype]  (HYPOTHESIS — validate with interviews)
One-liner: [age/role, what they sell, follower/revenue band]
Quote (illustrative): "[the pain in their words]"
Functional job / Emotional job / Social job
Objections: [the fear that blocks purchase]
PQL signal: [the usage moment that predicts they'll pay]
```
Mark invented quotes as illustrative — they're prompts for validation, not evidence.

## Trigger events (when they come looking)
List the concrete moments that push this ICP to search: a spike/overload, a bill
shock, a peer's disaster, a launch deadline, a seasonal push. Content should meet
each trigger.

## Watering holes (specific, not "social media")
Name exact subreddits, Discord/Skool/Circle communities, newsletters, podcasts,
and the influencers/educators whose audience IS your ICP. Educators currently
teaching an incumbent's tutorials are the highest-leverage partnership target.

## The ICP bet
Frame the chosen segment as a bet: what we believe, confidence, evidence, gaps,
30-day validation plan, risk if wrong.

## 30-day validation (the real next step)
Talk to 10. Ask about the past, never hypotheticals: "walk me through the last
time X happened," "what do you use now and why," "what would make you switch,"
"what breaks if it vanished." Kill signal: can't get 10 to talk, or no real pain.
